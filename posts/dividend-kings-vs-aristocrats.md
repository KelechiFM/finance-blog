This is the philosophy of **Dividend Growth Investing**.

While tech stocks grab the headlines with 20% daily swings, a quiet group of boring companies has been minting millionaires for decades. These are the **Dividend Aristocrats** and the **Dividend Kings**.

But what is the difference, and which one is right for your portfolio? Let's break down the battle of the passive income titans.

## Why This Strategy Wins

The magic isn't just the yield (the % you get paid today). The magic is the **Growth of Yield on Cost**.

Imagine you bought a stock at $100 paying a $3 dividend (3% yield).
Fast forward 20 years. That company is now a Dividend King. It has raised its dividend every year. Now, it pays $12 per share in dividends.
Your investment is still only the initial $100. You are now earning a **12% yield** on your original money, every single year, regardless of what the stock price does.

## Risks: The "Yield Trap"

New investors often make a fatal mistake: sorting by "Highest Yield."
They see a company paying 12% and think they found a goldmine. Usually, it's a landmine.

A yield that high often means the stock price has crashed because the business is failing. The dividend is likely to be cut soon.
**Rule of Thumb:** If a yield looks too good to be true (2x or 3x the market average), investigate the **Payout Ratio**. If a company is paying out 100% of its earnings to shareholders, that dividend is not safe.
